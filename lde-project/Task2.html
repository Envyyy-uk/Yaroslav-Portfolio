Reference from the Roster

“Empathy is an asset for entrepreneurs,” says Maria Ross, who founded Red Slice, a consulting firm that helps companies like Microsoft, Dropbox, and Talametry build brand stories. Ross advises corporations on how to change their internal policies and procedures to become truly empathetic [5].

American psychologists J. Salovey and J. Mayer (1990) introduced the term “emotional intelligence” to denote the degree of development of such human traits as self-awareness, self-control, motivation, change, putting oneself in the shoes of other people, finding mutual understanding with others. Is that it? [6]

Research shows that the quality of teamwork, especially the level of communication, directly affects the success of projects, both in academic and professional environments [7].

“Effective teamwork is impossible without individual responsibility, which begins with time management. The ability to overcome procrastination and complete tasks on time is the foundation for meeting deadlines for the entire team” [8].
